Technical workflow

One video from raw input to reviewed output

Processing layers

Audio layer FFmpeg extraction, non-stationary spectral gating, silence detection, acoustic disruption detection.
Speech layer ElevenLabs Scribe v2 word timestamps, Croatian transcript, sentence splitting, Gemini spell correction.
Decision layer RapidFuzz, multilingual sentence embeddings, Gemini duplicate checks, false-start and aside detection, enrichment arbiter.
Review layer Word-level keep/cut payload, reviewed edit plan, reviewed render with the raw source preserved.

Sequential pipeline

Processing stages

Each stage produces data used by later stages. Failures in enrichment fall back to the base edit plan so a video can still render.

1

Audio extraction and normalization

The raw video is probed for its first audio stream. Audio is extracted as mono 16-bit PCM WAV while preserving the source sample rate. The video stream remains the source for final rendering; this audio file is the analysis input.

ToolingFFmpeg and ffprobe.
OutputRaw mono WAV in the temporary workspace.
PurposeGive audio, speech, and disruption passes a stable input.
2

Noise reduction and silence map

The extracted audio is denoised with non-stationary spectral gating. The default reduction strength is conservative, so speech quality is prioritized over aggressive cleanup. FFmpeg silence detection then finds long low-volume regions. Silence regions are inverted into padded speech keep regions.

AlgorithmNon-stationary spectral gating via noisereduce.
Silence threshold-40 dB, minimum 3 seconds by default.
Speech padding500 ms before and after detected speech regions.
3

Speech-to-text and correction

The denoised audio is transcribed in Croatian with word-level timestamps. Audio-event tokens, such as cough or laughter markers, are kept separate from spoken words so they can inform editing without polluting transcript text. Sentences are built from timed words and split at long pauses. A Gemini spell-correction loop fixes obvious Croatian spelling errors without changing timestamps, style, grammar, or conversational filler.

STT modelElevenLabs Scribe v2, language hr, word timestamps.
Audio eventsEnabled by default and stored separately.
Correction modelGemini 2.5 Flash, structured output, up to 5 passes.
Sentence splitPunctuation plus pause split at 1.5 seconds by default.
4

Candidate cut detection

The edit decision layer generates cut candidates from multiple signals: silence, duplicate retakes, false starts, stutters, incomplete fragments, off-topic asides, and acoustic disruptions. The system favors precision: cheaper deterministic checks narrow the candidate set, and Gemini is used for judgment where context is needed.

Duplicate windowEach sentence compares against the next 5 sentences by default.
Lexical tierRapidFuzz ratio and token-sort ratio.
Semantic tierSentenceTransformer paraphrase-multilingual-MiniLM-L12-v2 cosine similarity.
LLM tierGemini 2.5 Flash for borderline pairs and context-heavy decisions.
5

EDL build and enrichment arbitration

Silence keep regions and cut flags are merged into one ordered edit decision list. Full-sentence flags remove sentence spans, while stutter flags can punch out sub-sentence word ranges. A separate enrichment pass scores every sentence as an independent keep/cut judgment. The arbiter can restore high-confidence wrong cuts and add conservative extra cuts for low-confidence asides, filler, incomplete thoughts, or tiny artifacts.

Enrichment modelGemini 2.5 Pro, low temperature, 20-sentence batches.
Status tiersGreen, yellow, red, restore.
Restore thresholdCut sentence with keep confidence at least 60 becomes a restore signal.
Arbiter restoreExisting full-sentence cut can be removed at keep confidence 70 or higher.
6

Automatic render

The automatic EDL is rendered into a first-draft edited MP4. Video comes from the raw source. Audio comes from the denoised WAV. Every keep segment is trimmed, timestamps are reset per segment, and the segments are concatenated. Short audio fades are applied at splice boundaries to reduce pops and harsh cuts.

RendererFFmpeg filter graph with trim, atrim, concat, and audio fades.
Default codeclibx264, CRF 28, ultrafast preset.
Splice smoothing30 ms audio fade in and fade out at segment boundaries.
StatusProduction render settings still need final validation.
7

Human review and reviewed render

The review payload exposes the raw video, timeline segments, transcript sentences, per-word keep state, reasons, confidence, enrichment tags, and salience scores. Reviewer changes are saved as word indices to cut. A reviewed EDL is built from the remaining word spans, then rendered as a separate reviewed MP4. The automatic EDL and raw recording remain intact.

Review granularityWord-level keep/cut state with sentence-level grouping.
Reviewed artifactSeparate reviewed EDL, not an overwrite of the automatic EDL.
Reviewed outputRendered with output suffix reviewed.

Decision orchestration

How keep and cut decisions are made

The pipeline is not a single AI call. It is a staged classifier that narrows candidates cheaply, uses embeddings for meaning, and uses Gemini where local context or editorial judgment is needed.

Duplicate retake detection

Sentence pairs are considered only within a local lookahead window. This avoids treating a later recap as a failed take.

  • RapidFuzz ratio and token-sort ratio identify exact or near-exact repetition.
  • Multilingual MiniLM embeddings catch paraphrased repetition.
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash judges borderline pairs with neighboring context and time gap.
  • Confirmed duplicate pairs are clustered so one sentence cannot be both kept and cut in different pair relationships.
  • Gemini chooses which confirmed version to keep, preferring completeness before clean delivery.
window size 5 lexical maybe 70 lexical definite 90 semantic maybe 0.75 semantic definite 0.95

False starts, stutters, and fragments

These passes handle material that is not always a full duplicate sentence. They combine local pattern detection with Gemini verification.

  • False starts between confirmed duplicate pairs are checked with Gemini against before and after context.
  • Intra-sentence stutters are detected by repeated 2-gram or 3-gram patterns, then Gemini chooses word ranges to cut.
  • Incomplete fragments are rule-prefiltered from punctuation, ellipses, and short filler-like wording, then verified by Gemini.
  • Word-level trims are represented as timed ranges, so the rest of the sentence can stay.
Gemini 2.5 Flash word-level trim fragment threshold 0.8

Asides and acoustic disruptions

Production noise and short off-lesson remarks are structurally different from duplicate retakes, so they have their own candidate path.

  • Short aside candidates must be near long silence or carry an audio-event marker.
  • Gemini answers whether the candidate is part of the lesson or an off-topic interruption.
  • Energy-based disruption detection looks for loud non-speech bursts inside gaps between transcribed words.
  • Audio false-start detection cuts short stranded phrases after a long pause and disruption when speech resumes promptly.
RMS frame 25 ms hop 10 ms noise floor pct 10 threshold +22 dB

Review payload semantics

Review is not a destructive video-editing session. It is a decision-editing session over timed transcript words and timeline segments.

  • Each word has original AI kept state, current kept state, reason, confidence, and keep score.
  • Each sentence has current action, original action, keep coverage, reason, note, status, tags, keep confidence, and rationale.
  • Reviewer changes are represented as the set of word indices to cut.
  • The reviewed EDL is rebuilt from contiguous kept word spans.
  • The reviewed render uses the same raw video and denoised audio path, but with reviewed decisions.
