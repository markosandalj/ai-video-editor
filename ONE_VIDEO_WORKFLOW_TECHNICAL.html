Historical architecture snapshot

This document predates the section editor becoming the default and still describes the retired enrichment arbiter and annotation queue. It is kept as historical context, not as runtime documentation. The current pipeline uses the section editor directly, rejects risky proposals, and reviews the EDL in the Transcript and Compare screens without a separate annotation pass.

Technical workflow

One video from raw input to reviewed output

Processing layers

Audio layer FFmpeg extraction, non-stationary spectral gating, silence detection, acoustic disruption detection.
Speech layer ElevenLabs Scribe v2 word timestamps, Croatian transcript, sentence splitting, Gemini spell correction.
Decision layer RapidFuzz, multilingual sentence embeddings, guardrailed LLM verification, deterministic keep-later take selection, false-start and aside detection, enrichment arbiter.
Review layer Web review app (FastAPI + React), word-level keep/cut payload, pipeline-vs-human diff view, reviewed edit plan, reviewed render with the raw source preserved.

Sequential pipeline

Processing stages

Each stage produces data used by later stages. Failures in enrichment fall back to the base edit plan so a video can still render.

1

Audio extraction and normalization

The raw video is probed for its first audio stream. Audio is extracted as mono 16-bit PCM WAV while preserving the source sample rate. The video stream remains the source for final rendering; this audio file is the analysis input.

ToolingFFmpeg and ffprobe.
OutputRaw mono WAV in the temporary workspace.
PurposeGive audio, speech, and disruption passes a stable input.
2

Noise reduction and silence map

The extracted audio is denoised with non-stationary spectral gating. The default reduction strength is conservative, so speech quality is prioritized over aggressive cleanup. FFmpeg silence detection then finds long low-volume regions. Silence regions are inverted into padded speech keep regions.

AlgorithmNon-stationary spectral gating via noisereduce.
Silence threshold-40 dB, minimum 3 seconds by default.
Speech padding500 ms before and after detected speech regions.
3

Speech-to-text and correction

The denoised audio is transcribed in Croatian with word-level timestamps. Audio-event tokens, such as cough or laughter markers, are kept separate from spoken words so they can inform editing without polluting transcript text. Sentences are built from timed words and split at long pauses. A Gemini spell-correction loop fixes obvious Croatian spelling errors without changing timestamps, style, grammar, or conversational filler.

STT modelElevenLabs Scribe v2, language hr, word timestamps.
Audio eventsEnabled by default and stored separately.
Correction modelGemini 2.5 Flash, structured output, up to 5 passes.
Sentence splitPunctuation plus pause split at 1.5 seconds by default.
4

Candidate cut detection

The edit decision layer generates cut candidates from multiple signals: silence, duplicate retakes, false starts, stutters, incomplete fragments, off-topic asides, and acoustic disruptions. The system favors precision: cheaper deterministic checks narrow the candidate set, an LLM verifies candidates where context is needed, and the final choices — which take survives, what is safe to auto-cut — are made by deterministic rules, not by the model.

Duplicate windowEach sentence compares against the next 5 sentences by default.
Lexical tierRapidFuzz ratio and token-sort ratio.
Semantic tierSentenceTransformer paraphrase-multilingual-MiniLM-L12-v2 cosine similarity.
LLM tierConfigurable cutting model (default Gemini 2.5 Flash) verifies borderline pairs only.
Take selectionDeterministic keep-later rule; the LLM no longer picks the surviving take.
Short-pair guardDefinite matches under 4 words are demoted to LLM review, never auto-cut.
5

EDL build and enrichment arbitration

Silence keep regions and cut flags are merged into one ordered edit decision list. Full-sentence flags remove sentence spans, while stutter flags can punch out sub-sentence word ranges. A separate enrichment pass scores every sentence as an independent keep/cut judgment. The arbiter can restore high-confidence wrong cuts and add conservative extra cuts for low-confidence asides, filler, incomplete thoughts, or tiny artifacts.

Enrichment modelGemini 2.5 Pro, low temperature, 20-sentence batches.
Status tiersGreen, yellow, red, restore.
Restore thresholdCut sentence with keep confidence at least 60 becomes a restore signal.
Arbiter restoreExisting full-sentence cut can be removed at keep confidence 70 or higher.
6

Automatic render

The automatic EDL is rendered into a first-draft edited MP4. Video comes from the raw source. Audio comes from the denoised WAV. Every keep segment is trimmed, timestamps are reset per segment, and the segments are concatenated. Short audio fades are applied at splice boundaries to reduce pops and harsh cuts.

RendererFFmpeg filter graph with trim, atrim, concat, and audio fades.
Default codeclibx264, CRF 28, ultrafast preset.
Splice smoothing30 ms audio fade in and fade out at segment boundaries.
StatusProduction render settings still need final validation.
7

Human review and reviewed render

Review happens in a local web app (FastAPI backend, React frontend): a waveform timeline synced to the video, the transcript with per-word keep state, and a review queue where every suggested cut carries its reason, confidence, and a plain-language rationale. The payload exposes reasons, enrichment tags, and salience scores; reviewer changes are saved as word indices to cut. A reviewed EDL is built from the remaining word spans and rendered from the same screen as a separate reviewed MP4. The automatic EDL and raw recording remain intact.

Review granularityWord-level keep/cut state with sentence-level grouping.
ExplanationsEach suggestion shows reason, confidence, and rationale; one-click restore.
Reviewed artifactSeparate reviewed EDL, not an overwrite of the automatic EDL.
Reviewed outputRendered from the review UI with output suffix reviewed.

Decision orchestration

How keep and cut decisions are made

The pipeline is not a single AI call, and the AI's role has been deliberately narrowed: it verifies and proposes, while deterministic rules — validated against 98 human-edited videos — make the final keep/cut choices such as which take survives a retake.

Duplicate retake detection

Sentence pairs are considered only within a local lookahead window. This avoids treating a later recap as a failed take.

  • RapidFuzz ratio and token-sort ratio identify exact or near-exact repetition.
  • Multilingual MiniLM embeddings catch paraphrased repetition.
  • The cutting LLM judges only borderline pairs, with neighboring context and time gap.
  • Definite pairs whose shorter sentence has fewer than 4 words are never auto-cut — short interjections ("Dobro.", "Ok.") are recurring discourse markers, not retakes.
  • Confirmed duplicate pairs are clustered so one sentence cannot be both kept and cut in different pair relationships.
  • Take selection is deterministic: the later take always survives (keep-later). The former Gemini "which to keep" pass is removed from the default path — on ground truth, human editors kept the later take in 71% of near-identical and 82% of paraphrase pairs, while sentence length carried no signal.
window size 5 lexical maybe 70 lexical definite 90 semantic maybe 0.75 semantic definite 0.95 take selection: last definite min words 4

False starts, stutters, and fragments

These passes handle material that is not always a full duplicate sentence. They combine local pattern detection with Gemini verification.

  • False starts between confirmed duplicate pairs are checked with Gemini against before and after context.
  • Intra-sentence stutters are detected by repeated 2-gram or 3-gram patterns, then Gemini chooses word ranges to cut.
  • Incomplete fragments are rule-prefiltered from punctuation, ellipses, and short filler-like wording, then verified by Gemini.
  • Word-level trims are represented as timed ranges, so the rest of the sentence can stay.
cutting LLM verification word-level trim fragment threshold 0.8

Asides and acoustic disruptions

Production noise and short off-lesson remarks are structurally different from duplicate retakes, so they have their own candidate path.

  • Short aside candidates must be near long silence or carry an audio-event marker.
  • Gemini answers whether the candidate is part of the lesson or an off-topic interruption.
  • Energy-based disruption detection looks for loud non-speech bursts inside gaps between transcribed words.
  • Audio false-start detection cuts short stranded phrases after a long pause and disruption when speech resumes promptly.
RMS frame 25 ms hop 10 ms noise floor pct 10 threshold +22 dB

Decision policy

AI proposes, deterministic rules decide

The pipeline has moved away from letting the model make final editorial choices. Wherever a rule measured against human editors beats LLM judgment, the rule wins — and every AI proposal passes deterministic validation before it becomes a cut.

Keep-later take selection

When a duplicate is confirmed, the later take always survives. The Gemini "which to keep" re-litigation pass was removed from the default path.

  • Measured on the fixture corpus: humans kept the later take in 71% of near-identical pairs and 82% of paraphrase pairs.
  • Keep-longer (completeness) carried no signal, so prefer_completeness is off by default.
  • A predictable rule professors can record for: flub, pause, redo clean — the earlier take is cut.
  • Removes an LLM call and a nondeterminism source per video.
take_selection last llm_keep_review off

Guardrails that outrank the model

Deterministic checks run after every LLM verdict, and can veto or demote it.

  • Definite similarity pairs under 4 words are demoted to review instead of auto-cut (protects recurring interjections).
  • Model confidence values are clamped to their valid range instead of trusted blindly.
  • Borderline verdicts below the confidence threshold (0.8) never become cuts.
  • Risky unique-content removals surface as review suggestions, not automatic cuts.
definite_min_words 4 confidence gate 0.8

Measured against human editors

Every decision-policy change is justified by data, not intuition: the QA harness scores the pipeline against 98 videos edited by human editors.

  • Human verdicts are derived from word-level coverage of the human editor's final video, not sentence matching.
  • Word-level precision/recall/F1 credits partial trims honestly.
  • A model-experiment runner benchmarks alternative LLMs (Gemini, GPT, Claude) via provider-agnostic manifests before any swap; eval-section-editor pilots the new architecture the same way.
  • A dev diff view overlays the pipeline's cuts and the human editor's cuts on the same raw transcript, so disagreements are visible at a glance.
  • LangSmith tracing can be enabled per environment to log every LLM call for debugging.
  • Failure patterns are catalogued in MISTAKE_PATTERNS.md and drive the roadmap.
98-video ground truth word-level scoring

Piloted architecture

Section editor: the LLM only names text, code makes the cut

An alternative cutting architecture, off by default behind section_editor.enabled, that pushes the same philosophy further: the model works purely on text and every proposal is deterministically verified.

Design

The tiered detector compares sentence pairs through a small model, one mechanism at a time, so it overcuts paraphrases and can only remove whole sentences. The section editor inverts this: one strong model reads paragraph-sized sections and proposes verbatim spans to delete.

  • Transcript is tiled into ~1200-word sections snapped to pause boundaries, with overlap context but disjoint ownership.
  • The model returns exact text spans tagged retake, false start, stutter, filler, or redundant — whole sentences or partial spans.
  • Verify-the-claim: a span that cannot be located verbatim in the sentence (match ratio ≥ 0.8) is rejected outright.
  • Text-to-timeline mapping is a separate deterministic step over existing word timestamps — the model never touches time.
  • Keep-later, recap time-gap (60 s), and short-interjection guardrails demote or reject risky retake deletions; "redundant" deletions always land as review suggestions.
off by default span match ≥ 0.8 protect < 4 words retake gap ≤ 60 s

Pilot results (10 fixtures, word-level scoring)

Compared against the tiered detector on the same fixtures, scored word by word against the human edit.

  • Cut F1 0.491 → 0.670 (+0.180 aggregate); 8 of 10 fixtures improved.
  • Cut precision 0.397 → 0.670: overcut words dropped 3,145 → 1,067 (−66%) at held recall.
  • Word-level trims the tiered detector could not express: one fixture produced 62 full-sentence cuts plus 39 partial trims.
  • Health telemetry (failed sections, rejected spans) prevents a broken model run from masquerading as a conservative editor.
  • Next: strong-model comparison (Gemini 2.5 Pro vs GPT-5.5 vs Claude) via eval-section-editor, then scale to all 98 fixtures before promotion.
F1 +0.180 overcuts −66%

Review payload semantics

Review is not a destructive video-editing session. It is a decision-editing session over timed transcript words and timeline segments.

  • Each word has original AI kept state, current kept state, reason, confidence, and keep score.
  • Each sentence has current action, original action, keep coverage, reason, note, status, tags, keep confidence, and rationale.
  • Reviewer changes are represented as the set of word indices to cut.
  • The reviewed EDL is rebuilt from contiguous kept word spans.
  • The reviewed render uses the same raw video and denoised audio path, but with reviewed decisions.
  • A separate diff endpoint renders the raw transcript with each editor's cuts struck through — pipeline decisions from the EDL, human decisions from word-level alignment to the edited video's re-transcription.
