Data source: per-sentence decision diffs generated by

```
uv run python -m ai_video_editor dump-alignments   # → output/alignments/<name>.alignment.{json,txt}
```

Every raw sentence is classified against the human edit: `true_keep`, `true_cut`,
`missed_cut` (human cut it, we kept it), `overcut` (we cut it, human kept it).
Raw totals across the corpus: **1,087 missed cuts, 176 overcuts**.

Classification rules used for the mining below (mirroring pipeline config):
a *twin* is another raw sentence with `max(fuzz.ratio, fuzz.token_sort_ratio) ≥ 70`
(= `lexical_maybe`); *in window* means within ±5 sentences (= `window_size`);
a *boundary artifact* is a missed/overcut sentence whose text is ≥85 partial-ratio
contained in the ground-truth text around its neighbours' matches.

## Pattern 0 (prerequisite): sentence-boundary artifacts — the eval counts noise as mistakes

**Not an editing mistake — a measurement bug. Fix this first or every number below is ~2× inflated.**

### Evidence
- **543 of 1,087 missed cuts (50%)** and **128 of 176 overcuts (73%)** are artifacts.
- 498 ground-truth sentences match no raw sentence at all (alignment blind spots).
- Example: `fizika25ljeto-002` row 24 is flagged `!MISS`, but rows 24–25 are two halves of
  a sentence the ground truth transcribed as one unit. The words were kept; only the
  sentence boundaries differ.

### Root cause
The ground truth is a *re-transcription* of the human's video, so ElevenLabs chunks the
same speech into different sentence boundaries. The human keep/cut verdict is derived by
sentence-level fuzzy alignment (`_align_monotonic` in
`ai_video_editor/qa/ground_truth.py`, threshold 65). A raw sentence whose words were
absorbed into a neighbouring GT sentence gets no match and is scored as "human cut it".

### Proposed fix
Derive the human verdict at the **word level**, not the sentence level:
1. Compute a word-level LCS between the full raw word sequence and the full GT word
   sequence (the LCS machinery already exists: `_lcs_length_table` / `_backtrack_lcs` in
   `ground_truth.py`; use `rapidfuzz.distance.LCSseq` or `difflib` if the O(n·m) table is
   too slow on long lectures).
2. A raw sentence counts as **human-kept** when ≥ ~60% of its words land in the LCS.
3. Use this verdict in `evaluate_decisions` (`qa/decision_eval.py`) and
   `build_alignment` (`qa/alignment.py`), keeping the sentence-level match only for
   attaching `gt_idx`/`gt_text` context.

### Expected impact / verification
Removes ~543 fake FN and ~128 fake FP. Aggregate cut recall should jump from ~0.45 to its
true value (~0.7 by rough estimate). Verify: re-run `dump-alignments` +
`eval-decisions`; the `fizika25ljeto-002` row-24 case must become `true_keep`, and
`gt_unmatched` counts should drop close to zero.

## Pattern 1: content cuts — human trims verbose meta-commentary we never consider (~300 misses, 55% of real misses)

### Evidence
Missed cuts with **no similar twin anywhere** in the transcript — not duplicates, not
retakes. The human removed them for pacing/quality. Recurring shapes:
- "Dakle, …" recap lead-ins re-stating what was just said
  (`engleski25ljeto-esej` rows 76, 106, 108, 109, 131)
- Re-explanations of an already-explained answer (`engleski25ljeto-listening-1` rows 16, 48, 61)
- Concentrated in the `engleski` reading/listening videos, where the human edits most
  aggressively (e.g. `engleski25ljeto-reading-1`: 27 of 37 real misses are content cuts).

### Root cause
The cutting pipeline is duplicate-shaped: every mechanism in
`duplicate/pipeline.py::detect_duplicates` (lexical/semantic/Gemini tiers, false starts,
stutters, fragments) needs either a repetition or a rule trigger. The one mechanism
designed for this — `holistic_redundancy_review` in `duplicate/gemini_verify.py` — is
exported from `duplicate/__init__.py` but **never called by the pipeline**. Its prompt is
also maximally conservative ("Budi VRLO konzervativan").

### Proposed fix
1. Wire `holistic_redundancy_review` into `detect_duplicates` as a final pass over kept
   sentences, gated by a confidence threshold in `DuplicateDetectionConfig`.
2. Tune its prompt with few-shot examples mined from the alignment dumps — the 300
   misclassified sentences *are* the training set. Categorise them (recap lead-in,
   answer re-explanation, meta-commentary) and show 2–3 real examples per category.
3. Start high-precision: enable at confidence ≥0.8, measure overcut movement, then relax.

### Expected impact / verification
Largest single recall win available (~55% of real misses). Watch cut precision closely —
this pass cuts unique content, so overcutting here is destructive. Track
`wrong_cut_by_reason` for the new mechanism in the QA report; require cut precision to
stay ≥0.85 on the fixture corpus before relaxing thresholds.

## Pattern 2: retakes inside the detection window that still got through (~111 misses, 20%)

The detector's core job, failed. Three distinct sub-patterns:

### 2a. Keep/cut inversions — we kept the take the human cut ✅ FIXED 2026-07-11
The pair *was* detected and cut, but the pipeline kept the opposite take from the human
(30 take disagreements in the corrected baseline). Root cause: the "which to keep" logic
preferred completeness > cleanliness > recency, re-litigated *every* confirmed pair via
`pick_best_version_with_gemini`, and used word count before recency in cluster survivor
selection.

**Measured policy accuracy** (pairs where the human kept exactly one side):

| policy | ≥90 sim (near-identical) | 70–90 sim (paraphrase) |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| keep-LATER | **51/72 right (71%)** | **157/192 right (82%)** |
| keep-LONGER | 11 right / 12 wrong / 49 tie | 107 right / 67 wrong (61%) |

Completeness carries no signal; recency is the human's dominant rule.

**Fix applied:**
- `llm_keep_review: bool = False` (new config) — the `pick_best_version_with_gemini`
  re-litigation pass is off by default (also removes an LLM call and a nondeterminism source).
- `prefer_completeness` default flipped to `False` — cluster survivors now tie-break by
  recency, not length.
- `DUPLICATE_PROMPT` keep criteria reordered: keep the LATER take by default; pick the
  earlier only when the later is *clearly* worse (interrupted, unfinished, more filler).

### 2b. Pairs at ≥90 similarity where NEITHER take was cut ✅ RESOLVED 2026-07-11
Replaying today's tier-1 lexical stage against the shipped EDLs found **51 definite pairs
(47% of all ≥90 pairs!) where both takes were kept**. They split into two populations:

1. **40 pairs: real retakes, human cut one side** (median shorter-side length 6 words).
   The fixture EDLs (generated 2026-06-13) are **stale** — today's tier-1 code flags all
   of these. No pipeline bug; regenerate the fixture EDLs to collect the recall win.
2. **10 pairs: short recurring interjections, human kept BOTH** ("Dobro.", "Ok.",
   "Zlatna metoda!" — 9 of 10 are ≤3 words, said 15–25s apart as discourse markers).
   Here the stale EDLs were accidentally right: today's code would have **auto-cut them
   with no Gemini review**, because sim=100 ≥ `lexical_definite` skips the tier that
   knows the "short phrases are duplicates only if consecutive" rule.

**Fix applied:** `definite_min_words: int = 4` (new config) — definite lexical/semantic
pairs whose shorter sentence has fewer words are demoted to the Gemini tier instead of
auto-cut. On the corpus this protects 9/10 kept-both pairs while only delaying (not
losing) 7/40 real retakes to Gemini review.

**Follow-up:** regenerate the fixture EDLs with the current pipeline (needs Gemini +
audio), then re-run `dump-alignments` — expect the `retake_in_window` bucket to shrink by
roughly the 40 stale-EDL pairs.

### 2c. Genuine detection misses — both takes kept, human kept one (sim 70–90) — OPEN
These reached the semantic/Gemini tiers and were rejected (or fell below
`gemini_confidence_threshold=0.8`). Now that 2a/2b are fixed, this is prompt/threshold
tuning on `DUPLICATE_PROMPT`; the keep-later measurement above (82% in this band) also
gives the prompt a stronger prior. Re-measure after the fixture EDLs are regenerated —
stale EDLs inflate this bucket too.

### Expected impact / verification
2a+2b together are ~85 misses plus 26 overcuts, all in the pipeline's core competency.
After each change, re-run `dump-alignments` and count `retake_in_window` misses and
inversions; they should drop mechanically, with no effect on content cuts.

## Pattern 3: fragments and interrupted thoughts (~78 misses, 14%)

### Evidence
Missed cuts that are ≤4 words or end in an ellipsis (speaker abandoned the thought):
- `engleski25ljeto-listening-2` [5]: `"..."` (1 word), [118]: `"Možemo prvo prevesti rečenicu."`
- `engleski25ljeto-listening-1` [176]: `"Da."`
- Longer ellipsis-terminated interruptions: `engleski25ljeto-esej` [74], [139]

### Root cause
Fragment detection is a rule pre-filter (`detect_fragment_candidates` in
`duplicate/redundancy.py`: trailing filler ≤5 words, content-subset, repeated-question)
plus Gemini confirmation gated at **confidence ≥0.8** (`pipeline.py`). The
`FRAGMENT_PROMPT` itself notes ellipsis endings are a near-certain cut signal, but such
sentences still need to pass the candidate rules first, and orphan interjections
("Da.", "...") carry real content words so `is_trailing_filler` misses them.

### Proposed fix
1. Add ellipsis-terminated sentences directly to the fragment-candidate list regardless
   of the other rules.
2. Add a rule for orphan micro-sentences (≤2 words) that are not answers to an on-screen
   question — let Gemini confirm with context as it already does.
3. Consider lowering the confirmation gate for ellipsis candidates only (0.8 → 0.6);
   the prompt already treats them as near-certain.

### Expected impact / verification
Small but cheap and low-risk (~14% of real misses). Verify via the `fragment` bucket
count in the mining, and watch `false_start` overcuts (currently the largest real overcut
mechanism at 102 raw / ~48 real) to make sure looser rules don't inflate them.

## Pattern 4: retakes beyond the detection window (~55 misses, 10%)

### Evidence
Missed cuts whose only twin is further than 5 sentences away. Median distance 44,
p90 = 79, max = 194 — but a cluster sits just past the window edge:
- `engleski25ljeto-listening-2` [317]→[323] (d=6, sim 72), [318]→[324] (d=6, sim 82)
- `engleski25ljeto-reading-1` [62]→[54] (d=8, sim 70)
- Long-range repeats are usually recurring phrases, not retakes:
  `engleski25ljeto-listening-2` [159]→[315] `"Zašto?"` (d=156, sim 100)

### Root cause
`window_size=5` bounds all pairwise comparison (`duplicate/windowed.py`). Retakes of
multi-sentence passages land the matching sentence 6–10 positions away. Distant repeats
(d>20) are mostly pedagogical recaps or recurring question phrases — the human sometimes
cuts them, but cutting them automatically risks destroying intentional structure (the
`DUPLICATE_PROMPT` explicitly treats large gaps as "keep both").

### Proposed fix
1. Cheap win: raise `window_size` from 5 to ~10. Cost is linear in window (lexical tier
   is rapidfuzz-fast); this catches the d=6–10 cluster.
2. Do **not** chase the long tail with a bigger window. If the d>20 cases matter later,
   add a separate transcript-global pass: embed all sentences, shortlist high-similarity
   distant pairs, and send only those to Gemini with the time-gap context it already uses
   to distinguish retake from recap. Treat short recurring questions ("Zašto?") as a
   stop-list.

### Expected impact / verification
The window bump alone should convert the d≤10 slice (~15–20 misses) with minimal risk.
Verify via the `retake_out_of_window` bucket and its distance distribution.

## Section-editor architecture (2026-07-11)

Inspired by the LLM-chaptering approach in the *Towards Data Science* article
("Automate Video Chaptering with LLMs and TF-IDF"): let the LLM work purely on text,
and make the text→timeline mapping a separate deterministic step. The article recovered
timestamps with fuzzy TF-IDF because it only had segment-level times; we have word-level
timestamps, so the mapping is exact.

### Why replace the tiered detector rather than keep patching it
The tiered detector (`duplicate/pipeline.py`) compares sentence *pairs* through a small
model, one mechanism at a time. That fragmentation is the root of the two dominant
failure modes measured above:
- it can only cut whole sentences (except the rule-gated stutter path), so it can't do the
  partial-sentence redo a human does instantly — hence 222 full-sentence `false_start`
  overcuts of near-verbatim-kept content, and missed partial redos;
- each pair-comparison prompt sees ±2 sentences, so it can't tell a recap from a retake —
  hence 228 paraphrase-duplicate overcuts and 224 content-cut misses.

A single strong model reading a whole section can judge both. So the section editor
*replaces* the text-judgment mechanisms; the audio lane (silence, disruptions, asides)
still runs alongside.

### Design (`duplicate/section_editor.py`, `SectionEditorConfig`, now the default)
LLM proposes, deterministic code disposes:
1. **Chunk** the transcript into ~1200-word sections snapped to pause boundaries.
   Ownership is disjoint; a few sentences of overlap give context so a retake straddling a
   boundary is visible without being double-cut (deletions are only accepted for the
   owning section).
2. **Ask** one strong model for verbatim spans to delete — whole sentences *or* partial
   spans — each tagged retake/false_start/stutter/filler/redundant, with the surviving
   twin index for retakes.
3. **Map** each span back to word indices via `SequenceMatcher`, producing word-level
   timestamps → the same `DuplicateFlag`/`WordTrim` objects the EDL and review UI already
   consume. Full-sentence coverage → whole cut; partial → word trim.

### The per-pattern fixes above become this system's guardrails
Everything proposed for the old pipeline survives as a deterministic check on the LLM's
proposals (not a patch on the tiered detector, which is bypassed when the section editor
is on):
- **verify-the-claim** → a span that can't be located verbatim (`min_span_match_ratio`) is
  rejected; the model may only delete text that exists.
- **keep-later** → a retake deletion that keeps the *earlier* take is rejected, so the
  proposed deletion stays in the video.
- **recap time-gap** → retake deletions whose twin is >`retake_max_gap_s` away are
  rejected (the 10s-vs-25s separation measured in Pattern 2a).
- **short-interjection protection** (`protect_min_words`) → whole-sentence retake cuts of
  <4-word lines ("Dobro.") are rejected, mirroring `definite_min_words`.
- **reject risky cuts** (`reject_types`) → `redundant` (unique-content) deletions are
  rejected rather than emitted, per the Pattern 1 rule that risky content cuts should
  not auto-apply without a dedicated review queue.
